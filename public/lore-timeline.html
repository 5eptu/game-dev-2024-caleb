World Building Lore Timeline

8 May 1945 - Germany Surrenders
Key Decision: Adolf Hitler's death led to a power vacuum.
Allied Advances: The Allies' successful campaign in Europe culminated in Germany's surrender.
2 Sep 1945 - Japan Surrenders
Atomic Bombings: The bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki pressured Japan into surrender.
Military Overhaul: Japan restructured its military leadership following the war.
24 Oct 1945 - The United Nations is founded
Global Cooperation: Established to prevent future global conflicts.
Charter Signing: The UN Charter was signed by 51 nations.
9 Feb 1946 - Stalin’s Speech, Cold War Begins
Ideological Divide: Stalin's rhetoric solidified the East-West divide.
Nuclear Tensions: The arms race escalated as both sides built nuclear arsenals.
12 Mar 1947 - Truman Doctrine
Containment Policy: The U.S. pledges to support countries resisting communism.
Military Aid: Financial aid was provided to Greece and Turkey.
4 Apr 1949 - The North Atlantic Treaty Organization is ratified
Mutual Defense: NATO members agree to mutual defense in case of attack.
Expansion: New members joined, increasing NATO's influence.
1 Oct 1949 - Mao takes over China, turning it communist
Civil War: The Chinese Civil War culminated in the establishment of the People's Republic of China.
Social Changes: Land reforms and communist policies were instituted.
June 24 1950 - Korean War begins
Invasion: North Korea invaded South Korea, igniting the conflict.
Global Involvement: The U.N. intervened on behalf of South Korea.
1 Aug 1952 - InkTech established
Technology Leader: InkTech rapidly developed military technologies.
Innovative Strategies: Pioneered advancements in computing and communication.
5 Oct 1953 - The Korean War ends in a United Nations victory
Armistice: A ceasefire was declared, solidifying the division of Korea.
Political Tensions: The war left a lasting impact on U.S.-North Korean relations.
5 Sep 1953 - SCP Foundation established
Secret Operations: Established to contain and study anomalies.
Global Influence: The SCP Foundation grew in secrecy, influencing global events.
14 May 1955 - The Treaty of Friendship, Cooperation and Mutual Assistance (Warsaw Pact) is formed
Counterbalance: Formed in response to NATO, solidifying Eastern bloc alliances.
Military Cooperation: Enhanced military collaboration among member states.
20 Jul 1969 - Apollo 11 lands on the Moon
Historic Achievement: First humans set foot on the Moon, showcasing U.S. space superiority.
Space Race: Further escalated competition in the space race between superpowers.
1970 to 1989 - The Cold War continues
Nuclear Proliferation: Both sides developed and tested more nuclear weapons.
Espionage: Increased intelligence operations and covert activities.
9 Nov 1989 - Fall of the Berlin Wall
Symbol of Unity: The wall's fall signified the end of the Cold War.
Reunification: Germany began the process of reunifying.
15 Mar 1990 - Dissolution of the Soviet Union
End of an Era: The Soviet Union officially dissolved, the Russian Empire was formed as a result.
Independence Movements: Many former Soviet republics gained independence.
18 Mar 1990 - Snowkid Sr. begins a movement in the United States called the ‘Frost’
A severe worldwide economic crisis was in affect.
Snowkid Sr. gained support by promising the people change, gaining rapid opinion.
3 Jan 1994 - NATO moves in to secure the former satellite states and move them towards capitalism and democracy.
This flushed out communism from former soviet rule.
The Red Scare event has dissolved.
6 Jan 1994 - The Russian Empire invades Finland, the ‘Third’ Russo-Finnish War Begins 8 Jan 1994 - NATO and the United States support Finland.
The Russian Empire launches a large-scale military offensive into Finnish territory, marking the start of the 'Third' Russo-Finnish War.
NATO and the United States pledge military and logistical support to Finland, signaling a global response to the Russian aggression.
29 Sep 1995 - The ‘Frost’ Movement spread to Canada.
The Frost Movement spreads to Canada, resonating with the public's desire for reform.
As economic hardships intensify, Canadian citizens begin rallying behind the Frost Movement, with protests and rallies demanding significant governmental and economic changes.
18 Aug 1996 - Peace terms are negotiated and the Treaty of Madison is signed, the war ends as the Russian Empire annexes part of the Nordico Republic.
The Treaty of Madison is formalized, bringing an end to the conflict. The Russian Empire gains partial control over the Nordico Republic, solidifying their annexation.
As the news of the treaty spreads, international reactions are mixed. While some nations condemn the annexation, others see it as a necessary step toward stability in the region.
10 Feb 1999 - The United Nations is dissolved.
Amid growing geopolitical tensions and internal divisions, the United Nations officially announces its dissolution, marking the end of an era of global diplomacy.
Some nations immediately seek to form new alliances, while others fall into political disarray.
11 Jun 1999 The North Atlantic Treaty Organization is dissolved.
Following the disbandment of the United Nations, NATO faces increasing internal discord and is officially dissolved, marking the end of the longstanding military alliance.
With NATO gone, former member states scramble to reassess their defense strategies, leading to a surge of new, hastily formed regional alliances and a period of military uncertainty.
28 Aug 1999 - The United States economy crashes and anarchy follows and Canada’s economy falls.
The U.S. economy crashes, triggering widespread chaos. Markets plummet, unemployment skyrockets, and civil unrest begins to spread across major cities.
As a direct consequence of the U.S. collapse, Canada’s economy falters. The interconnectedness of their economies leads to a similar downturn, resulting in a wave of panic and instability throughout the region.
18 Jul 2000 - The ‘Frost’ movement grows greatly and takes control of the eastern United States and eastern Canada.
The 'Frost' movement gains significant momentum, seizing control of key cities in the eastern United States. Their influence spreads rapidly, capitalizing on the economic collapse and civil unrest.
Following their U.S. success, the Frost movement extends its control into eastern Canada, solidifying its hold over the region and establishing a strong presence along the Atlantic coast.
25 Dec 2000 - The Frost Shield is formed in the eastern United States and Canada, this new nation’s economy surpasses the United States.
On Christmas Day, the Frost movement officially forms a new nation known as "The Frost Shield" across the eastern United States and Canada.
Within days of its formation, the Frost Shield's economy surpasses the remaining United States, becoming a major economic force in North America amidst the ongoing global instability.
4 Jul 2001 - The United States’ and Canada’s economy recover, the United States and Canada recognize The Frost Shield as an independent nation
On Independence Day, both the United States and Canada see their economies begin to recover from the collapse.
Just days after the economic recovery, the United States and Canada formally recognize the Frost Shield as an independent and sovereign nation, solidifying its status on the world stage.
11 Sep 2001 - Al-Qaeda flies 4 planes into the World Trade Center, Pentagon, and the United States Capitol Building
Al-Qaeda orchestrates a coordinated attack on the United States by hijacking planes and flying them into the World Trade Center and the Pentagon, causing widespread devastation.
A fourth plane, also hijacked, is aimed at the United States Capitol Building but is thwarted by the passengers on board, crashing into a field in Pennsylvania instead.
14 Sep 2001 - As the United States declare war on Al-Qaeda, they drive a car bomb into The Frost Shield Presidential Housing Building, killing Snowkid Sr.. His son, Snowkid, takes power and declares war on Al-Qaeda, the Good War begins.
A car bomb is detonated outside the Presidential Housing Building in The Frost Shield, resulting in the assassination of Snowkid Sr., the leader of the nation.
Following the assassination of his father, Snowkid assumes leadership of The Frost Shield and promptly declares war on Al-Qaeda, initiating the conflict known as the Good War.
20 Dec 2002 - The Frost Shield paratroop into a Al-Qaeda compound and take Osama bin Mohammed bin Awad bin Laden, they are evaced via helicopter
The Frost Shield conducts a daring operation, deploying paratroopers into an Al-Qaeda stronghold to capture Osama bin Laden.
After successfully apprehending bin Laden, Frost Shield forces are evacuated from the compound via helicopter, marking a significant victory in the Good War.
8 May 2003 - Al-Qaeda surrenders and dissolves, the Good War ends.
Following extensive military pressure and loss of key leadership, Al-Qaeda officially surrenders.
The surrender leads to the formal conclusion of the Good War, with the Frost Shield emerging as a prominent force in the region and setting the stage for a new era of stability.
9 May 2003 - Lim3Z is given The Frost Shield Medal of Honor by Snowkid
In recognition of his bravery and contributions during the Good War, Lim3Z is awarded The Frost Shield Medal of Honor by President Snowkid.
The Frost Shield holds a nationwide celebration in honor of Lim3Z's achievements and the end of the Good War.
28 Nov 2005 - Lim3Z becomes The Frost Shield’s Vice Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff
Lim3Z is appointed as The Frost Shield’s Vice Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, where he plays a crucial role in shaping military strategy and operations.
Following Lim3Z's appointment, significant military reforms are initiated to modernize the armed forces, emphasizing technology and training to better prepare for future challenges.
8 Jan 2008 - The Russian Empire attempts to invade Alaska, but The Frost Shield pushes back against them, The Frost Shield declares war and the Russo-Frost War begins.
The Russian Empire attempts to invade Alaska, aiming to reclaim territory lost during previous conflicts.
In response to the invasion, The Frost Shield declares war, marking the official start of the Russo-Frost War, mobilizing troops and resources to defend their territory.
15 Aug 2008 - The Frost Shield lands in Russia and pushes in
The Frost Shield launches a surprise attack, successfully landing troops in Russian territory, marking a significant escalation in the Russo-Frost War.
As The Frost Shield forces push deeper into Russia, they aim to secure vital supply lines and weaken the Russian military's hold in the region.
25 Dec 2008 - The Frost Shield is stopped by the Russian Winter but airstrikes continue
The Frost Shield's advance is significantly slowed as they face severe winter conditions in Russia, forcing troops to regroup and reassess their strategy.
Despite the ground forces being stalled, the Frost Shield maintains pressure through strategic airstrikes on key Russian positions, aiming to weaken their defenses and disrupt supply lines.
1 Jan 2009 - The Frost Shield forces are pushed to the sea, the Communist Party of Italy wins the election and Italy becomes the Socialist-Communist Republic of Italy (SCRI)
The Frost Shield is forced to retreat to the coast as Russian forces launch a counteroffensive, effectively pushing the coalition back to their naval positions.
In a significant political shift, the Communist Party of Italy wins the general election, leading to the establishment of the Socialist-Communist Republic of Italy (SCRI).
4 Jan 2009 - Israel declares war on Palestine, the Isreali-Palestinian War begins
Israel declares war on Palestine, citing security concerns and ongoing hostilities.
The declaration of war leads to a rapid escalation in violence, prompting international reactions and concerns over a wider conflict in the Middle East, as both sides prepare for a protracted struggle.
5 Jan 2009 - The United States attempts to make peace between Israel and Palestine.
The United States initiates diplomatic talks aimed at brokering peace between Israel and Palestine, seeking to reduce tensions and prevent further escalation of the conflict.
As the U.S. intervenes, neighboring countries express varying levels of support for both sides, complicating the peace efforts and highlighting the regional implications of the ongoing war.
26 Mar 2009 - The Frost Shield’s assault on the Russian Empire
The Frost Shield begins a series of coordinated airstrikes targeting key military installations within the Russian Empire, aiming to weaken their defenses and gain an upper hand in the ongoing Russo-Frost War.
Following the airstrikes, Frost Shield ground forces initiate a major offensive, moving deeper into Russian territory in an effort to secure strategic positions and disrupt enemy supply lines.
7 Aug 2009 - The Frost Shield officially calls on The Russian Empire to surrender, they do not.
The Frost Shield issues an official ultimatum to the Russian Empire, demanding their immediate surrender and the withdrawal of troops from occupied territories, emphasizing the consequences of continued resistance.
The Russian Empire rejects the ultimatum, declaring that they will fight to the last man, reinforcing their commitment to the war and vowing to reclaim any lost territories.
15 Dec 2009 - The Russian Winter stops The Frost Shield again.
The Russian Winter intensifies, bringing heavy snow and frigid temperatures that hinder The Frost Shield's mobility and operations on the ground, forcing them into a defensive posture.
In light of the severe winter conditions, The Frost Shield's military leaders convene to reassess their strategy, focusing on supply lines and planning for a potential spring offensive once the weather improves.
19 Dec 2009 - Israel accepts the peace terms established by the United States
A ceasefire is officially established, leading to a temporary halt in hostilities between Israel and Palestine, creating a window for peace negotiations.
Following the acceptance of peace terms, several nations express their support for the agreement, paving the way for future diplomatic recognition of Palestine.
2 Apr 2010 - The Frost Shield gives fake information and flies hundreds of fake planes over Russia and crashes them in the night with paratroopers in them.
The Frost Shield executes a covert operation, deploying hundreds of fake planes to create the illusion of a massive air assault on Russia, aiming to confuse and distract Russian military forces.
While the fake planes create chaos, actual paratroopers are deployed to infiltrate strategic locations in Russia under the cover of night, seeking to gather intelligence and disrupt enemy operations.
3 Apr 2010 - The Russian Empire rushes to the crash sites and are ambushed by the airborne troops
As Russian forces scramble to respond to the crash sites, they fall into a well-coordinated trap set by the Frost Shield’s airborne troops, leading to heavy casualties among the surprised Russian soldiers.
The Frost Shield’s airborne troops leverage the element of surprise, seizing key positions and disrupting Russian supply lines.
7 Apr 2010 - The Frost Shield lays out surrender terms for the Russian Empire.
The Frost Shield presents an ultimatum to the Russian Empire, outlining terms for an end to hostilities that include territorial concessions and political reforms.
The Russian Empire deliberates on the Frost Shield’s terms, facing internal pressure and potential dissent as public opinion sways amidst the ongoing conflict.
8 Apr 2010 - The Russian Empire accepts the surrender terms and the Treaty of Juneau is signed, The Frost Shield annexes eastern Russian territory and is paid trillions.
The Treaty of Juneau is officially signed, marking the end of the Russo-Frost War. The agreement outlines the annexation of eastern Russian territory by the Frost Shield.
The Russian Empire agrees to compensate the Frost Shield with trillions in reparations, significantly boosting the Frost Shield's economy.
14 Mar 2012 - Snowkid and Lim3Z attend a special meeting with France, Great Britain, Spain, Portugal, Canada, United States, Poland, and Germany.
Snowkid and Lim3Z participate in a high-stakes diplomatic summit with leaders from France, Great Britain, Spain, Portugal, Canada, the United States, Poland, and Germany to discuss global security and trade relations.
During the meeting, several bilateral agreements are reached, strengthening ties between the Frost Shield and European nations.
14 Mar 2014 - The Frost Shield, France, Great Britain, Spain, Portugal, Canada, United States, Poland, and Germany create the North Atlantic Trade Line.
The Frost Shield, along with France, Great Britain, Spain, Portugal, Canada, the United States, Poland, and Germany, officially establishes the North Atlantic Trade Line to enhance economic cooperation and streamline trade processes among member nations.
Member nations agree on a unified set of economic policies aimed at reducing tariffs and improving trade logistics, which significantly boosts economic growth across the region and strengthens political alliances.
25 Apr 2014 - The economies of The Frost Shield, France, Great Britain, Spain, Portugal, Canada, United States, Poland, and Germany fall due to improper trade.
The Frost Shield and its partners, including France, Great Britain, Spain, Portugal, Canada, the United States, Poland, and Germany, experience a significant economic downturn attributed to improper trade practices and unsustainable economic policies.
In response to the crisis, an emergency economic summit is called, gathering leaders from all member nations to devise strategies for stabilizing their economies and restoring public trust in the trade agreement.
30 Jul 2014 - Snowkid loses the election and secretly gives the order to assassinate the winner.
The Frost Shield experiences a wave of protests and riots as citizens demand justice and transparency regarding the circumstances surrounding the leader’s death. Tensions rise between Snowkid's supporters and opposition groups, threatening to destabilize the nation.
As investigations into the assassination begin, Snowkid strategically utilizes propaganda to frame the situation as a necessary action for national security. He mobilizes loyalists within the military and government to suppress dissent and strengthen his grip on power, presenting himself as the only figure capable of leading The Frost Shield through the ensuing chaos.
2 Aug 2014 - The winner is assassinated, Snowkid stays in power.
The newly elected leader of The Frost Shield is assassinated under mysterious circumstances, leading to public unrest and speculation about the motives behind the sudden death.
With the assassination successfully executed, Snowkid consolidates his power once again, reinstating his leadership and reaffirming his authority over The Frost Shield amidst growing tension and distrust among the populace.
 3 Apr 2015 - An intelligence officer leaks the assasination order to the press.
The leak of the assassination order sends shockwaves through The Frost Shield and the international community. As news outlets publish the story, protests erupt across the nation, with citizens demanding accountability from Snowkid’s administration. Calls for an independent investigation grow louder, intensifying the already volatile political climate.
Snowkid's government enacts a series of emergency measures aimed at quelling opposition. Authorities conduct raids on suspected dissenters, and media outlets that report on the leak face censorship and threats. Snowkid’s regime employs a combination of propaganda and intimidation to maintain control, framing the unrest as a threat to national security.
4 Apr 2015 - Anti-Snowkid protest and riots begin
What starts as peaceful demonstrations in major cities quickly escalates into widespread riots as crowds demand Snowkid's resignation. Protesters clash with law enforcement, who respond with tear gas and rubber bullets. The chaos spreads to smaller towns, with citizens taking to the streets to voice their discontent against the government's actions.
In the wake of the protests, various opposition groups begin to organize, uniting factions that have previously acted independently. These groups aim to present a coordinated front against Snowkid's regime, advocating for democratic reforms and accountability. They utilize social media to mobilize supporters, raising awareness of their cause both domestically and internationally.
2 Sep 2015 - Snowkid is killed outside The Frost Shield Capitol by rioters.
As Snowkid leaves a public event at The Frost Shield Capitol, a large crowd of angry protesters gathers outside. Amidst the chaos, a faction of rioters breaks through security barriers, leading to a confrontation. Snowkid is fatally injured.
Following Snowkid's assassination, the nation is thrown into further chaos as news of his death spreads. Supporters mourn, while opponents celebrate. The government declares a state of emergency, imposing curfews and mobilizing military forces to maintain order. To temporarily restore order until a replacement was found, Lim3Z would take the spot.
25 May 2016 - The Economy is revived and Lim3Z steps down from his position and Dong Chau takes his place.
Lim3Z spearheads initiatives aimed at reducing unemployment and inflation, leading to a gradual recovery. Key industries are revitalized, and international trade agreements are renegotiated, boosting investor confidence.
As the economy begins to show signs of recovery, Lim3Z announces his decision to step down as Vice Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff. In a ceremonial event, he officially hands over power to Dong Chau, emphasizing the importance of new leadership to navigate the challenges ahead.
