8 May 1945 - Germany Surrenders

End of WWII in Europe

Germany’s surrender marks the official end of World War II in Europe. Allied forces triumph after years of fierce battles, leading to the collapse of the Nazi regime.

Shift to Post-War Recovery

This surrender paves the way for the division of Germany into occupation zones and the eventual Cold War, as the ideological divide between the Allies and the Soviets becomes apparant.

2 Sep 1945 - Japan Surrenders

End of World War II

Japan’s surrender officially ends World War II, after the devastating atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.

U.S. Occupation and Reconstruction

After Japan’s surrender, the U.S. leads a military occupation of Japan, initiating major political, social, and economic reforms, which reshape Japan into a modern state.

24 Oct 1945 - The United Nations is founded

Formation of the UN

The United Nations is established as an international body aimed at maintaining peace, international cooperation, and preventing future world conflicts.

Successor to the League of Nations

The UN replaces the League of Nations, which failed to prevent World War II, with a more robust structure designed to handle conflicts and promote global diplomacy.

9 Feb 1946 - Stalin’s Speech, Cold War Begins

Ideological Conflict Begins

Stalin’s speech is often viewed as the starting point of the Cold War, as it emphasized the incompatibility of communism and capitalism, escalating tensions between the USSR and the West.

Breakdown of Wartime Alliances

The speech is the growing rift between former Allies, with the Soviet Union and the Western powers entering a period of distrust and geopolitical rivalry.

12 Mar 1947 - Truman Doctrine

U.S. Policy of Containment

The Truman Doctrine signifies the United States’ commitment to containing the spread of communism, particularly in Greece and Turkey, marking the start of U.S. Cold War foreign policy.

Financial and Military Aid

Under this doctrine, the U.S. provides financial and military aid to countries threatened by communism, laying the foundation for later interventions during the Cold War.

4 Apr 1949 - The North Atlantic Treaty Organization is ratified

NATO’s Formation

NATO is established as a military alliance between North American and European nations, designed to provide collective security against the Soviet Union.

A Collective Defense Strategy

The core principle of NATO is mutual defense; an attack against one member is considered an attack against all, creating a united front against possible Soviet aggression.

1 Oct 1949 - Mao Takes Over China, Turning It Communist

Establishment of the People's Republic of China

Mao Zedong declares the establishment of the People's Republic of China, following the defeat of the Nationalist forces in the Chinese Civil War.

A New Power in the Communist Bloc

The creation of communist China shifts the balance of power in Asia, as it becomes a key ally of the Soviet Union, increasing Cold War tensions.

24 Jun 1950 - Korean War Begins

North vs. South Korea

The Korean War begins with North Korea’s invasion of South Korea, backed by Soviet and Chinese support, against the South, backed by the United States and UN forces.

Cold War Proxy Conflict

The Korean War is one of the first major military conflicts of the Cold War, setting the stage for future proxy wars between communist and capitalist states.

1 Aug 1952 - InkTech Established

Foundation of a Megacorporation

InkTech is founded with the aim of developing technologies, eventually growing into a corporate giant with significant influence across multiple sectors.

Early Developments and Expansion

Initially, InkTech focuses on ink-based technologies, but its ambitions quickly expand into various fields such as bioengineering, military applications, and consciousness research.

5 Oct 1953 - The Korean War Ends in a United Nations Victory, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea is Annexed into the Republic of Korea

Unified Korea

Following a decisive United Nations victory, North Korea is annexed into South Korea, forming a single Korean state under a democratic government.

Long-Term Impact on Cold War Politics

The end of the Korean War shifts the balance in Asia, with Korea becoming a staunch ally of the West, while tensions between China and the Soviet Union rise due to their defeat.

14 May 1955 - The Warsaw Pact is Formed

Soviet Response to NATO

The Warsaw Pact is established as a collective defense treaty between the Soviet Union and its Eastern European satellite states, forming a military alliance to counter NATO.

Division of Europe

The formation of the Warsaw Pact solidifies the division of Europe into Western capitalist and Eastern communist blocs, further entrenching Cold War rivalries.

20 Jul 1969 - Apollo 11 Lands on the Moon

First Human Landing on the Moon

Apollo 11, a U.S. space mission, successfully lands humans on the moon, with Neil Armstrong becoming the first person to set foot on its surface, marking a historic achievement for humanity.

Cold War Space Race Victory

The moon landing represents a major victory for the United States in the Cold War space race, showcasing its technological superiority over the Soviet Union.

1970 to 1989 - The Cold War Continues

Proxy Wars and Political Maneuvering

The Cold War intensifies with various proxy wars (e.g., Vietnam, Afghanistan), espionage, and political competition between the superpowers.

Arms Race and Détente

This period sees an ongoing arms race, including the development of nuclear weapons, but also moments of détente, where tensions temporarily ease.

15 Mar 1990 - The Soviet Union Forms into the Russian Empire

Collapse of the Soviet Union

The Soviet Union officially dissolves, and in its place, the Russian Empire is formed, marking a significant geopolitical shift at the end of the Cold War.

Return of Monarchical Rule

With the formation of the Russian Empire, a new form of leadership emerges, taking a central role in the post-Soviet state.

18 Mar 1990 - Snowkid Sr. Begins a Movement in the United States Called the ‘Frost’

Rise of the Frost Movement

Snowkid Sr. initiates the 'Frost' movement, a quiet yet profound call for a societal shift towards progress through unity. His vision promotes technological advancement, efficiency, and the betterment of humanity through innovation and order.

Influence on U.S. Environmental Policy

The Frost movement quickly garners support from citizens eager for change, captivated by Snowkid Sr.'s clear vision of a society driven by merit, cooperation, and sustainable growth. His ideas are seen as hope for a more organized and harmonious future.

2 Jan 1994 - The Russian Empire pulls out of Germany, Poland, and other satellite states

Subsection 1

The Russian Empire withdraws its military forces, making the end of decades-long occupation in these regions. Former satellite states regain full sovereignty, paving the way for political and economic reforms.

Subsection 2

Countries like Poland and Germany begin transitioning towards democratic governance and market economies. The pullout allows NATO to extend its influence further into Eastern Europe, setting the stage for future geopolitical shifts.

3 Jan 1994 - NATO moves in to secure the former satellite states and move them towards capitalism and democracy

Subsection 1

Following the Russian Empire's withdrawal, NATO forces are swiftly deployed to the former satellite states to ensure stability and security. NATO collaborates with local governments to facilitate a transition from authoritarianism to democracy. Western powers encourage the adoption of free-market principles, guiding these nations towards capitalism through structural reforms and international investments.

Subsection 2

Alongside political and economic changes, there is a growing push for cultural integration with the West, leading to stronger ties with institutions like the European Union.

6 Jan 1994 - The Russian Empire invades Finland, the ‘Third’ Russo-Finnish War Begins

Subsection 1

The Russian Empire launches a large-scale invasion of Finland, marking the start of the Third Russo-Finnish War. Finland scrambles its forces to resist the offensive and protect its borders.

Subsection 2

NATO and Western countries swiftly condemn the invasion, while Sweden and Norway prepare for the possibility of Russian expansion into Scandinavia, raising concerns of a broader conflict.

29 Sep 1995 - The ‘Frost’ Movement spreads to Canada

Subsection 1

The Frost Movement, a revolutionary establishment group, begins to gain traction in major Canadian cities, advocating for radical changes in governance and economic structure.

Subsection 2

The Canadian government, caught off guard by the movement's popularity, implements emergency measures and increases security presence in urban areas to prevent potential unrest.

18 Aug 1996 - Peace terms are negotiated and the Treaty of Madison is signed, the war ends as the Russian Empire annexes part of the Nordico Republic.

Subsection 1

After intense negotiations, the Treaty of Madison is signed, officially ending the Third Russo-Finnish War. The agreement allows the Russian Empire to annex a significant portion of the Nordico Republic, cementing its dominance in the region.

Subsection 2

The treaty sparks widespread international criticism, with Western powers condemning the Russian Empire's expansionist ambitions. However, NATO refrains from military involvement, focusing instead on diplomacy.

11 Jun 1999 The North Atlantic Treaty Organization is dissolved

Subsection 1

The decision follows growing internal disagreements and a shifting world order after the end of the Cold War and the rise of new global powers.

Subsection 2

The dissolution of NATO creates a power vacuum in the defense landscape, leading to increased uncertainty among former member states. Many nations begin to reconsider their defense strategies, alliances, and the future of international security cooperation.

28 Aug 1999 - The United States economy crashes and anarchy follows and Canada’s economy falls

Subsection 1

The U.S. economy suffers a catastrophic crash, leading to widespread financial ruin. Stock markets plunge, major industries crumble, and mass unemployment sets in. The crash sparks social unrest, resulting in chaos and the breakdown of government structures in many regions. Anarchy begins to spread throughout the country.

Subsection 2

Closely tied to the U.S. economy, Canada’s financial system collapses shortly after. The nation experiences widespread economic distress, with major businesses shutting down, leading to unemployment and a humanitarian crisis.

18 Jul 2000 - The ‘Frost’ movement grows greatly and takes control of the eastern United States and eastern Canada

Subsection 1

The ‘Frost’ movement gains significant momentum, appealing to disillusioned citizens seeking stability amidst the chaos of the economic collapse. Their ideology spreads rapidly, attracting support from various factions in the population who are desperate for change.

Subsection 2

The movement successfully seizes control over major cities and regions in the eastern United States and eastern Canada. As local governments struggle to maintain order, the ‘Frost’ movement establishes its own governance structures, promoting its vision of a new socio-political order amidst the anarchy.

25 Dec 2000 - The Frost Shield is formed in the eastern United States and Canada, this new nation’s economy surpasses the United States’

Subsection 1

The ‘Frost’ movement officially declares the establishment of The Frost Shield, a new nation uniting the eastern United States and Canada under its banner. This move is seen as a direct response to the collapsing governments in these regions.

Subsection 2

The Frost Shield’s economy rapidly surpasses that of the former United States, driven by a combination of resource control, innovative governance, and the support of its populace. The new nation leverages its stability and effective leadership to attract investment and trade, establishing itself as a significant player on the North American stage.

4 Jul 2001 - The United States’ and Canada’s economy recover, the United States and Canada recognize The Frost Shield as an independent nation

Subsection 1

Following years of turmoil, both the United States and Canada implement a series of reforms that stabilize their economies. These reforms focus on rebuilding infrastructure, encouraging investment, and restoring public confidence in financial institutions, leading to a gradual resurgence in economic activity.

Subsection 2

In a significant diplomatic shift, the United States and Canada officially recognize The Frost Shield as an independent nation. This acknowledgment facilitating trade agreements and cooperative efforts in security and governance, despite the complex history of the regions involved.

11 Sep 2001 - Al-Qaeda flies 4 planes into the World Trade Center, Pentagon, and the United States Capitol Building

Subsection 1

Two planes are hijacked and flown into the Twin Towers of the World Trade Center in New York City, causing massive destruction and loss of life. The impact results in the collapse of both towers, symbolizing a devastating attack on American economic and cultural power. A third plane crashes into the Pentagon, the headquarters of the United States Department of Defense, leading to significant casualties and further highlighting the vulnerability of national security. This coordinated assault aims to inflict fear and chaos, altering the course of U.S. foreign and domestic policy.

Subsection 2

A fourth plane is hijacked with the intended target of the United States Capitol Building in Washington, D.C. However, brave passengers attempt to retake control, leading to the plane crashing in a field in Pennsylvania, preventing further destruction and loss of life.

14 Sep 2001 - As the United States declare war on Al-Qaeda, they drive a car bomb into The Frost Shield Presidential Housing Building, killing Snowkid Sr.. His son, Snowkid, takes power and declares war on Al-Qaeda, the Good War begins.

Subsection 1

A car bomb is detonated outside The Frost Shield Presidential Housing Building, resulting in the assassination of Snowkid Sr., the leader of The Frost Shield. This violent act not only eliminates a key figure in the region but also escalates tensions between The Frost Shield and the United States.

Subsection 2

Following his father's death, Snowkid ascends to power and takes immediate action against the perpetrators of the attack. He declares war on Al-Qaeda, framing it as a response to both the assassination and the global threat posed by the terrorist organization. This marks the beginning of the "Good War," which aims to eradicate terrorism and secure the safety of The Frost Shield and its allies.

20 Dec 2002 - The Frost Shield paratroop into a Al-Qaeda compound and take Osama bin Mohammed bin Awad bin Laden, they are evaced via helicopter

Subsection 1

In a coordinated military operation, paratroopers from The Frost Shield launch an assault on a known Al-Qaeda compound, utilizing advanced tactics and technology to infiltrate the heavily guarded area. The operation aims to capture high-value targets involved in terrorist activities.

Subsection 2

During the raid, The Frost Shield forces successfully locate and apprehend Osama bin Mohammed bin Awad bin Laden, the notorious leader of Al-Qaeda. Following his capture, the paratroopers execute a swift evacuation via helicopter, ensuring that bin Laden is transported securely back to The Frost Shield territory for interrogation and trial. This significant victory boosts the morale of The Frost Shield and is seen as a turning point in the ongoing conflict against terrorism.

8 May 2003 - Al-Qaeda surrenders and dissolves, the Good War ends

Subsection 1

Facing overwhelming military pressure and the strategic victories achieved by The Frost Shield and its allies, Al-Qaeda officially announces its unconditional surrender.

Subsection 2

Following the surrender, Al-Qaeda is formally dissolved as an organization, with key leaders captured or eliminated during the conflict. The remaining members are disbanded, and efforts begin to reintegrate former militants into society. The end of the Good War is celebrated as a triumph for The Frost Shield and a step towards stabilizing the region.

9 May 2003 - Lim3Z is given The Frost Shield Medal of Honor by Snowkid

Subsection 1

In a ceremony attended by high-ranking officials and citizens of The Frost Shield, Snowkid honors Lim3Z for his exceptional bravery and contributions during the Good War.

Subsection 2

The Frost Shield Medal of Honor is presented to Lim3Z as a symbol of valor, sacrifice, and dedication to the nation. This recognition not only elevates Lim3Z's status as a national hero but also serves as an inspiration for others to serve and protect their homeland.

28 Nov 2005 - Lim3Z becomes The Frost Shield’s Vice Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff

Subsection 1

Lim3Z is appointed as the Vice Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff for The Frost Shield, reflecting his outstanding military career and leadership skills. This position places him at the forefront of military strategy and national defense, overseeing operations and advising the Chairman on key military decisions.

Subsection 2

In his new role, Lim3Z prioritizes the modernization of The Frost Shield’s military forces, advocating for advancements in technology, training, and intelligence capabilities. He emphasizes the importance of preparing for emerging threats and forming strong alliances with other nations, ensuring The Frost Shield remains a formidable power in global security.
