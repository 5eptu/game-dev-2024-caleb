8 May 1945 - Germany Surrenders
Germany officially surrenders, marking the end of World War II in Europe.
The Nuremberg Trials (1945-1946)
The Nuremberg Trials held Nazi leaders accountable for war crimes.
2 Sep 1945 - Japan Surrenders
Japan surrenders after the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki, marking the end of World War II.
Atomic Bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki (6 & 9 Aug 1945)
The United States dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki, causing mass destruction and leading to Japan's surrender.
Occupation of Japan (1945-1952)
The Allied occupation of Japan began, with reforms and demilitarization led by General Douglas MacArthur.
24 Oct 1945 - The United Nations is founded
The United Nations is established to promote international cooperation and prevent future conflicts.
San Francisco Conference (25 Apr - 26 Jun 1945)
Representatives from 50 countries met to draft the UN Charter.
Universal Declaration of Human Rights (10 Dec 1948)
The UN adopts the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, establishing a global standard for human rights.
9 Feb 1946 - Stalin’s Speech, Cold War Begins
Stalin's "Election Speech" indicated the Soviet Union's opposition to capitalism, marking the beginning of Cold War tensions.
The Iron Curtain Speech (5 Mar 1946)
Winston Churchill famously warned of the Soviet "Iron Curtain" descending across Europe, solidifying Cold War divisions.
